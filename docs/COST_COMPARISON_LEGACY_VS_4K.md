# Cost Comparison: 540p vs 1080p vs 4K

**Scenario**: 50 Events/Year, 300 Clips/Event, 20s/Clip (5,000 mins/year total).

| Line Item | **540p (Legacy)** | **1080p (Pro)** | **4K (Premium)** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **System Reliability** | � Unstable | 🟢 Reliable | 🟢 Reliable |
| **Encoding (Input)** | Free | $150 / year | $500 / year |
| **Storage (Month 1)** | $12 / mo | $15 / mo | $48 / mo |
| **Storage (Month 12)** | $144 / mo | $180 / mo | $576 / mo |
| **Total Year 1 Cost** | **~$936** | **~$1,320** | **~$4,256** |

*(Note: "Total Year 1 Cost" = One-time encoding + Cumulative Storage for growing library)*

## The Breakdown
1.  **540p (Legacy)**: Cheapest (~$900/yr), but video looks pixelated and server crashes on upload.
2.  **1080p (Pro)**: The "Sweet Spot". Only **~$400 more** than 540p per year, but enables Direct Upload (No crashes) and great quality.
3.  **4K (Premium)**: Provides "Master Quality" but costs **~$3,300 more** per year than 540p.

## Recommendation
**Start with 1080p (Plus Tier)**.
*   It solves the crash/upload problem.
*   It looks professional.
*   It saves you ~$3,000/year compared to 4K.
*   You can upgrade to 4K later if users demand it.
