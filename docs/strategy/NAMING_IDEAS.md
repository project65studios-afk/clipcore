## 🧠 How Real Startups Choose Names (The Framework)
User requested insight on how "real software businesses" do this.

### Lane 1: Descriptive (Safe & Clear)
**"It does exactly what it says on the tin."**
*   *Examples*: Salesforce, PayPal, FaceBook, **VideoReflect**.
*   *Pros*: Zero marketing needed. Customers get it instantly.
*   *Cons*: Hard to trademark. Can feel generic. **(Stick here for B2B/Photographers)**.

### Lane 2: Suggestive (The "Vibe")
**"It hints at the benefit or feeling."**
*   *Examples*:
    *   **Slack**: (Cut me some slack / less stress).
    *   **Stripe**: (A simple magnetic stripe / straightforward).
    *   **Zoom**: (Fast / movement).
*   *Our Contenders*: **Momentum** (Moving forward), **Flux** (Flow).

### Lane 3: Abstract (The Empty Vessel)
**"A made-up word that becomes a brand."**
*   *Examples*: Uber, Kodak, Google.
*   *Pros*: You own the trademark easily.
*   *Cons*: Requires millions in ads to teach people what it means. **(Avoid this).**

### The "Bar Test" 🍺
Can you say the name in a loud bar and have the person understand it?
*   "I work at **VideoReflect**." -> "Oh, like PhotoReflect but for video?" (Pass)
*   "I work at **Kinetix**." -> "Kine-what? Genetics?" (Fail)
